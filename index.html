The short version

The big, scary launch work is done and verified in code: Supabase auth, Sign in with Apple, the RevenueCat paywall and an enforced subscription gate, the Founding-Circle seat counter wired to a real DB count, account deletion that actually wipes Storage and cancels the RevenueCat customer, all Info.plist permission strings, Sentry crash reporting, and the iPad V1 layer. The invite-join ghost-member bug (#115) is fixed in code too.

What's actually left splits into three honest buckets: (1) a handful of Apple-rejection and legal/admin gates that block submission, (2) experience gaps that decide whether the first 100 families stay — the biggest of which is the one Gabby named: reminders don't fire and the solo-user reward loop doesn't make sense, and (3) a long, healthy roadmap of fast-follows and native-iOS ideation. The two most important non-Apple things before launch are notification-permission priming (a cold deny permanently kills the reminders three users just asked for) and a working solo reward model (the thing that made Gabby call it "boring").

2 · Should do before launch

decides whether the first 100 stay

None of these will get you rejected. All of them shape whether a Founding Circle family activates and keeps using it. Ordered by impact. The top two are the direct answer to Gabby.

Notification-permission priming screen

Not builtC

The app currently calls the iOS permission prompt cold. A cold deny is near-permanent and silently kills the reminders three users just asked for. A warm "here's why" screen before the system prompt is the single highest-leverage retention fix. Promote toward must if reminders are core to launch.

Reminders — ship the quick slice now

Not built#180C

The full bucket system (Morning/Afternoon/Evening) is blocked on THINGS.md Thread 3 (the "This week" lane, itself half-built) — that's a fast-follow. But the morning-reminder time-setter is independent: the function and DB write path already exist, nothing exposes a picker. One settings row makes a daily reminder actually fire. Directly answers Gabby/Michele/Sarah.

Solo reward model — make the loop make sense

Decision + build#187 follow-onC · J

The reward backbone shipped (#187), but for a self-funding solo user the loop is hollow — "I can reward myself anytime." Two forks you discussed with Gabby: (a) self-discipline — she writes her own rule and the app holds her to it, or (b) accountability partner — add a supporter who gates the treat until she hits a star threshold. See Decisions below. This is the "boring" root cause; worth solving for the solo launch segment.

Analytics (PostHog or Amplitude)

Not builtC · J

No product analytics at all. You can't learn from the first 100 without it. Subject-respecting events only (no PII, no copy of state values). Disclose in Privacy Policy + App Privacy label.

Day-1 warmth (not just a tour)

PartialC

Starter tasks + the CompanionWelcome tour exist, but a new user lands on a functional task screen, not a "you're safe here, today is day 1" moment. The checklist's demo-confetti pre-completed task isn't built. This is the emotional first 5 minutes.

Accessibility pass on core surfaces

PartialC · Ext

Reduce Motion is solid. Gaps: VoiceOver labels are sparse on home / task / state check-in; Dynamic Type untested for layout breakage; no per-theme color-contrast audit on record. Important for this audience specifically.

StoreKit review prompt

Not builtC

No native review prompt. Low-risk lever — wire it to fire only after a positive moment (post-approval, non-shutdown day, after first payout). Never day 1, never after a hard moment.

Support link in main Settings

PartialC

A support@ link lives in outer-ring settings only. Add an "report an issue / get help" link to the main caregiver + subject Settings so help is reachable during onboarding.

Two known bugs worth clearing

Open#163 · #164C

#163 stale join-state can trap a signed-in user at onboarding finish. #164 verify the Lexend trailing-character clip is Expo-Go-only and not in EAS builds. Both small, both touch first-run polish.

Decisions only you can make

I'll build the moment you pick
1 · Solo reward model (highest impact — Gabby's "boring") Self-discipline (she writes her own rule, app holds her to it) vs accountability partner (a supporter gates the treat until a star threshold) vs both/either, user's choice. This reshapes the solo reward surface that shipped yesterday.
